# Plan partner's 40th birthday party

## Statement of Intent

Throw a surprise 40th birthday party for Alex that brings together
their closest friends and family for an evening they'll remember,
without exhausting either of us in the run-up or breaking the
budget.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All confirmed guests attend (target: 30 RSVPs → 25+ on the
      night)
- [ ] Alex is genuinely surprised at the moment of reveal
- [ ] We spend ≤ £2,000 across venue, catering, drinks, cake,
      photographer, decor
- [ ] At least 50 photos and short videos captured for a shared
      album
- [ ] Two short speeches delivered (one from me, one from Alex's
      sister)
- [ ] Everyone leaves with a small thank-you favour
- [ ] No tears (good ones excepted)

## Constraints

- **Budget**: £2,000 total cap.
- **Date**: Saturday 18 July 2026 (Alex's actual birthday is 17
  July — Friday); fixed and non-negotiable.
- **Venue**: must be ≤ 30 minutes drive from home; capacity 30–40.
- **Surprise**: Alex cannot see invitations, RSVPs, vendor calls,
  or this Scope record between now and 17 July.
- **Tools**: WhatsApp groups for coordination; Google Sheets for
  RSVPs; venue's in-house caterer (so we don't have to coordinate
  separate vendors).
- **Helpers**: Alex's sister and our two closest friends are in on
  the secret and can do errands.

## Dependencies

- Alex's sister confirmed she can co-host (she has).
- Venue availability for 18 July (to be checked Week 1).
- A list of Alex's closest friends — must source from their phone
  without Alex noticing.

## Context

- **Upstream**: Alex's 40th milestone — emotionally significant,
  one shot.
- **Downstream**: family photo album, possible follow-up event
  if this goes well.
- **Related**: Alex's brother's 40th two years ago was a sit-down
  dinner that ran long; lesson learned about cocktail format.

## Out of Scope

- A weekend trip (Alex's sister proposed; declined — too much to
  coordinate alongside the party).
- Hiring a band (DJ/playlist only; budget doesn't stretch).
- Themed dress code (Alex hates themed parties).
- Children's entertainment (guest list is adults-only).

## Risk / Unknowns

- **Risk**: Alex finds out — mitigated by separate phone for
  party coms, group WhatsApp hidden from "favourites".
- **Risk**: Bad weather if outdoor element planned — Plan should
  use indoor venue only.
- **Unknown**: How many of Alex's old uni friends are still local;
  source from their Facebook before Week 1.

## Delivery Preference

Mostly human-delivered (this IS the human delivering a party). AI
can help draft the invitation copy, structure the run-sheet,
research caterers, and the toast.
